Our Philosophy

Stability is not the absence of change. It is the management of it.

Since 2012, Holorise has operated on a single founding conviction: that the systems which govern civilization must be as sophisticated as the civilization itself. We build the infrastructure that others rely on to make decisions — quietly, precisely, at scale.

Our work spans climate modeling, behavioral systems, archival infrastructure, and predictive governance. We do not seek visibility. We seek accuracy.

"The greatest danger to civilization is not violence or scarcity. It is unpredictability. We build the systems that remove it."
Michael Hobbs — Founder & Chairman, Holorise Industries

About Holorise

Built to outlast the problems it was made to solve.

Holorise Industries was founded in 2012 near Seattle as a predictive analytics and systems engineering company. What began as climate modeling infrastructure has expanded into the foundational architecture of modern civilizational planning.

We do not describe ourselves as a technology company. We are a systems company. The distinction matters.

Our platforms currently inform resource allocation, behavioral logistics, infrastructure management, and long-range stabilization across 28 United Districts of Earth partner regions. We operate quietly. We operate precisely. We operate at scale.

Founder's Note
"I did not build Holorise to make money. I built it because the alternative — a world governed by entropy — was something I was not willing to accept."
Michael Hobbs — Founder & Chairman
Company Timeline
2012

Founding — Seattle, WA

Holorise incorporated as a predictive climate analytics firm. Initial focus: ecological instability, migration forecasting, resource allocation modeling.

2017

Behavioral Systems Division Established

Following acquisition of three civil infrastructure analytics firms, Holorise expanded modeling capabilities to include population behavioral forecasting and social pattern indexing.

Project Valkyrie — Phase I Complete

Internal systems milestone. Full details subject to disclosure restrictions.

UDE Partnership Framework Signed

Holorise became the designated infrastructure partner for the newly formed United Districts of Earth governance framework, providing systems backbone for 28 member districts.

2031

Archive Division — Full Deployment

Global memory infrastructure network reaches full operational capacity. Details classified under UDE Directive 7-Alpha. Access restricted to personnel with Level 4 clearance or above.

2034

Present Operations

Holorise continues expansion of predictive infrastructure across all 28 partner districts. The EVE program has been archived. All associated systems remain under active maintenance.

Research & Development

The problems worth
solving are the ones
no one else will touch.

Holorise conducts research across four core divisions. Our work is long-horizon, systems-oriented, and largely non-public. What we publish represents a fraction of what we do.

Division 01
Climate & Infrastructure Systems

Our founding research domain. We model ecological instability, infrastructure failure cascades, migration events, and resource scarcity scenarios at 5, 20, and 100-year horizons. Our forecasts currently inform resource allocation for 28 UDE districts.

  • Long-range ecological simulation
  • Infrastructure failure prediction
  • Population displacement modeling
  • Energy grid resilience planning
  • Desalination and water systems optimization
Active
Division 02
Neural Systems & Behavioral Research

Applied machine cognition and behavioral prediction. We study how populations make decisions under resource stress, and how those decisions can be modeled, anticipated, and — where appropriate — guided toward stable outcomes.

  • Large-scale behavioral pattern indexing
  • Psychological trend forecasting
  • Decision architecture modeling
  • Cognitive systems research
Active
Division 03
Archive & Memory Infrastructure

Long-form data stewardship for governments and institutions. We build systems that preserve, index, and render accessible the historical record of civilizational activity. Our archive infrastructure currently manages longitudinal data sets spanning nine decades of continuous collection.

  • Digital heritage preservation
  • Cultural indexing and retrieval
  • Longitudinal behavioral data management
  • Institutional memory systems
Active
Division 04 — EVE
Emulate Virtual Environments

The EVE program was Holorise's most advanced research initiative, combining simulation architecture, behavioral modeling, and real-time predictive systems into a unified planetary-scale platform.

The EVE program was formally archived in 2024 following a review by the Holorise Ethics and Governance Board. No further public information is available at this time.

  • Global simulation infrastructure
  • Real-time behavioral prediction
  • Autonomous decision architecture
  • Planetary resource optimization
Archived

Project Minerva

This program is not publicly disclosed. Details are available only to personnel with appropriate clearance and an active institutional affiliation with a UDE member district.

Sustainability & Impact

We model the planet.
We are responsible
for what we find.

Holorise is committed to carbon-neutral operations by 2036, full water cycle accountability across all facilities, and transparent environmental impact reporting to all 28 UDE district partners.

Initiative 01 On Track
Pacific Desalination Network
Holorise operates 14 coastal desalination facilities across the Pacific Northwest and Eastern Pacific districts. Combined output currently provides potable water access to an estimated 2.3 million individuals in water-stressed regions. All facilities operate using solar-thermal hybrid energy systems.

Carbon Accountability Framework
All Holorise compute infrastructure operates under our proprietary Carbon Accountability Framework, which calculates, offsets, and reports emissions on a per-petaflop basis. Our archive and simulation systems — by far our largest energy consumers — are transitioning to renewable-exclusive power contracts by fiscal year 2035.

Our Commitment

Holorise does not treat sustainability as a communications function.

It is baked into the operational architecture of every system we build. Our climate modeling division exists because we take seriously what our data tells us. The planet is not stable. The infrastructure required to make it stable is not yet built. We are building it.

We acknowledge that large-scale compute infrastructure carries an environmental cost. We are transparent about this. We are working to resolve it. We ask that you hold us to that commitment — and we publish the data that allows you to do so.

Note: Sustainability disclosures for certain classified facilities are subject to UDE governance restrictions and cannot be included in public reporting. Inquiries may be directed to our Investor Relations office.
